What Makes a Song Popular

Let’s unpack what actually made a song popular in the 1980’s. What elements of the music were important for it to be liked by the masses throughout a whole decade? To explore this, I ran a stepwise regression on all the audio features Spotify provided for the Top 100 songs of the 1980’s, with popularity as the dependent variable.

After running the analysis, we are left with the following co-efficients for a regression formula:

\[ popularity = 79.29 + (-.0.09)(energy) + (-0.02)(duration) + (-0.08)(acousticness) + (0.41)(speechiness) \] This equation lets us make the inference that in the 1980’s, the audio features of a track that contributed to it being popular were energy, duration, acousticness and speechiness. More specifically, a song being lower in energy, shorter in duration, lower in acousticness and higher in speechiness would make it more popular.
